# Productivity & Decision Frameworks

High-leverage prompts based on proven mental models: 80/20, fear-setting, minimum effective dose.

---

## The 80/20 Analyzer (Pareto on Steroids)

Apply the Pareto principle at three levels to find what actually matters.

```
Analyze my current [WORK/BUSINESS/LIFE AREA]: [DESCRIBE YOUR SITUATION].

Apply the 80/20 principle at 3 levels:
1) What 20% of activities produce 80% of my results?
2) Within that 20%, what 20% produces 80% of THOSE results (the 4%)?
3) What 80% should I eliminate or delegate immediately?

Give me a specific action plan to focus only on the vital few.
```

**Variables:** `[WORK/BUSINESS/LIFE AREA]`, `[SITUATION]`
**Tags:** `productivity`, `pareto`, `prioritization`, `80-20`

## The Fear-Setting Framework (Worst-Case Scenario Planner)

Based on Tim Ferriss's fear-setting exercise. Use when paralyzed by a big decision.

```
I'm considering [BIG DECISION/CHANGE] but I'm paralyzed by fear.

Walk me through Tim Ferriss's fear-setting exercise:
1) What's the worst that could happen if I do this? (Be specific)
2) How could I prevent each worst-case scenario?
3) How could I repair the damage if it happens?
4) What's the cost of inaction over 6 months, 1 year, 3 years?

Make this analysis brutally honest.
```

**Variables:** `[BIG DECISION/CHANGE]`
**Tags:** `decision-making`, `fear`, `tim-ferriss`, `worst-case`

## The Minimum Effective Dose (MED) Calculator

Find the simplest path to 80% of the result — and eliminate everything else.

```
I want to achieve [SPECIFIC GOAL] but I'm overcomplicating it.

What's the absolute minimum effort/time/resources needed to get 80% of the desired result?

Break this down into:
1) The ONE thing that would make the biggest impact
2) What I can eliminate without losing results
3) A minimalist daily/weekly routine to maintain progress

Make it so simple a lazy person would actually do it.
```

**Variables:** `[SPECIFIC GOAL]`
**Tags:** `productivity`, `simplicity`, `MED`, `minimalism`
