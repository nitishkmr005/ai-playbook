# Executive Workflows

High-leverage prompts for managers and leaders. Sourced from Satya Nadella's reported daily AI workflow with GPT.

Source: r/ChatGPTPromptGenius

---

## 1. Meeting Prep That Actually Works

Predicts what someone wants to discuss using your interaction history — no more awkward "so what did you want to cover?" moments.

```
Based on my prior interactions with [PERSON], give me 5 things likely top of mind for our next meeting.
```

**Variables:** `[PERSON]`

---

## 2. Project Status Without the BS

Pulls from actual communications rather than sugar-coated updates — gives the real picture.

```
Draft a project update based on emails, chats, and all meetings in [SERIES/PROJECT]: KPIs vs. targets, wins/losses, risks, competitive moves, plus likely tough questions and answers.
```

**Variables:** `[SERIES/PROJECT]`

---

## 3. Reality Check on Deadlines

Asks for a probability instead of a vague status — forces honest assessment.

```
Are we on track for the [PRODUCT] launch in [DATE]? Check eng progress, pilot program results, risks. Give me a probability.
```

**Variables:** `[PRODUCT]`, `[DATE]`

---

## 4. Time Audit

Reveals where time actually goes vs. where you think it goes.

```
Review my calendar and email from the last month and create 5 to 7 buckets for projects I spend most time on, with % of time spent and short descriptions.
```

---

## 5. Never Get Blindsided Again

Turns your AI into a briefing assistant that knows the full context of ongoing conversations.

```
Review [SELECT EMAIL] + prep me for the next meeting in [SERIES], based on past manager and team discussions.
```

**Variables:** `[EMAIL]`, `[SERIES/PROJECT]`

---

## Notes

These prompts work best when the AI has access to your actual communication data (via integrations like Microsoft 365 Copilot, Notion AI, or similar). Without that context, substitute by pasting the relevant emails/notes directly into the prompt.

The pattern: use AI as a **digital chief of staff** that synthesizes across your entire workspace — not just answers one-off questions.
